Sisig

A Filipino Food

a Filipino dish made from pork jowl and ears (maskara), pork belly, and chicken liver, which is usually seasoned with calamansi, onions, and chili peppers. It originates from the Pampanga region in Luzon.

Ingredients in Cooking a Sisig:

  • Pork: Traditionally, pig's face, ears, and liver are used. However, some variations use pork belly, shoulder, or a combination of different pork cuts.
  • Calamansi or lemon juice: Provides acidity and brightness to the dish.
  • Onion: Adds sweetness and texture.
  • Chili peppers: Gives heat and spice to the dish.
  • Soy sauce: Adds saltiness and depth of flavor.
  • Mayonnaise: Some modern versions include mayonnaise to add creaminess to the dish.
  • Garlic: Adds aroma and flavor.
  • Salt and pepper: To season the dish to taste.
  • Optional garnishes: Green onions, chopped cilantro, and crispy fried garlic are often used as garnishes to enhance the flavor and presentation.

Steps in Cooking a Sisig:

  1. Prepare the Ingredients:

    Clean and prepare the pork by washing it thoroughly. If using pig's face and ears, boil them until tender, then grill or broil to achieve a crispy texture. If using pork belly or shoulder, dice them into small pieces. Finely chop onions, garlic, and chili peppers. Slice calamansi or lemon for juice.

  2. Cook the Pork:

    If using pig's face and ears, grill or broil them until crispy, then chop into small pieces. If using pork belly or shoulder, heat a pan over medium heat and cook the diced pork until browned and crispy. Remove excess fat if desired.

  3. Saute Aromatics: In a separate pan, heat some oil over medium heat. Add chopped onions and garlic, and sauté until fragrant and translucent.
  4. Combine Pork and Aromatics: Add the cooked pork to the pan with the sautéed aromatics. Mix well to combine.
  5. Finish Cooking: Cook the pork mixture until heated through and well combined with the seasonings. You can cook it for a few more minutes to allow the flavors to meld together.
